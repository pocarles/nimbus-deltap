Why Nitrogen?

Compressed air works, but nitrogen works better. Here's why professionals choose N₂.

Pressure Stability

Nitrogen maintains consistent pressure over time. No gradual loss, no constant re-checking.

Humidity Insensitive

Unlike air, nitrogen is dry. No moisture means no internal corrosion and predictable behavior in any weather.

Consistent Damping

Stable gas properties mean your suspension performs the same on a cold morning or a hot afternoon.

Longer Component Life

Nitrogen is inert — no oxidation, no seal degradation. Your shocks last longer.

What's the output pressure range?

0–50 bar (0–725 PSI), adjustable via the precision bleed valve. The gauge shows both bar and PSI scales for convenience.

How many shock adjustments per cartridge?

Each cartridge holds ~54 liters of nitrogen at atmospheric pressure. Depending on your shock size and pressure requirements, you can typically perform 15-30+ full adjustments per cartridge.

Why nitrogen instead of compressed air?

Nitrogen is dry (no moisture), stable (maintains pressure over time), and inert (won't corrode seals). Compressed air contains moisture that can cause inconsistent damping and internal corrosion. Every professional race team uses nitrogen — now you can too.
